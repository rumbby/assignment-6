Goan Cuisine

A delicious blend of Portuguese and Indian flavors

Goan Fish Curry

The quintessential Goan dish made with coconut milk, tamarind, and spices. Best enjoyed with rice.

Spice Level: Medium Best at: Fisherman's Wharf, Panjim

Prawn Balch√£o

A spicy and tangy pickle-like dish made with prawns, vinegar, and a fiery red chili paste.

Spice Level: High Best at: Ritz Classic, Panjim

Pork Vindaloo

Fiery curry with Portuguese roots, made with pork, vinegar, garlic, and chili peppers.

Spice Level: High Best at: Mum's Kitchen, Panjim

Sorpotel

A complex, multi-layered pork dish cooked with liver, heart, and spices, often served at feasts.

Spice Level: Medium Best at: Martin's Corner, Betalbatim

Vegetable Xacuti

A rich coconut-based curry with a complex spice blend, typically made with mixed vegetables.

Spice Level: Medium Best at: Gunpowder, Assagao

Bebinca

Goa's famous layered pudding cake made with coconut milk, eggs, and ghee.

Spice Level: Mild Best at: Confeitaria 31 de Janeiro, Panjim

Feni

Goa's distinctive spirit distilled from cashew apples or coconut palms.

Alcohol Content: High Best at: Local taverns across Goa
